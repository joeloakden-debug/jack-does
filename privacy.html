privacy policy

last updated: March 29, 2026

Jack Does ("Company," "we," "us," or "our"), located at 1511 Southview Street, Coquitlam, BC, Canada, V3E 0E3, is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal and financial information. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our website, client portal, and related services (collectively, the "Service").

By using the Service, you consent to the data practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with the practices described herein, please do not use the Service.

1. information we collect

1.1 information you provide to us

We collect information that you voluntarily provide when using the Service, including:

  • Account information: your name, email address, phone number, and business name
  • Financial documents: bank statements, credit card statements, receipts, invoices, and other financial records you upload through the client portal
  • Accounting data: transaction details, account balances, chart of accounts, and other financial data accessed through your QuickBooks Online account when you authorize the connection
  • Communications: messages, questions, and other content you submit through the "Ask Jack" chat feature or other communication channels
  • Payment information: billing details necessary to process payments for our services

1.2 information collected automatically

When you access the Service, we may automatically collect certain information, including:

  • Device information: browser type, operating system, and device identifiers
  • Usage data: pages viewed, features used, time spent on the Service, and interaction patterns
  • Log data: IP address, access times, and referring URLs

1.3 information from third parties

When you authorize a connection to QuickBooks Online or other third-party platforms, we receive financial data from those services as necessary to provide our accounting services. This may include transaction history, account balances, customer and vendor lists, and other accounting data.

2. how we use your information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Providing services: to perform bookkeeping, accounting, tax preparation, and financial reporting services on your behalf
  • Document processing: to analyze, categorize, and reconcile financial documents you upload
  • AI-assisted analysis: to use artificial intelligence tools to assist with document analysis, transaction categorization, and data extraction, with all outputs reviewed by qualified human professionals
  • Communication: to respond to your inquiries, provide financial insights through the chat feature, and send service-related notifications
  • Service improvement: to understand how our Service is used and to improve its functionality and user experience
  • Legal compliance: to comply with applicable laws, regulations, and professional standards, including those set by the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)

3. how we share your information

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal or financial information to third parties. We may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

  • QuickBooks Online (Intuit Inc.): when you authorize a connection, we exchange data with QuickBooks Online to perform bookkeeping and accounting services. This data exchange is governed by Intuit's Privacy Statement
  • AI service providers: we use AI tools (including Anthropic's Claude) to assist with document analysis. Financial data shared with these services is used solely for processing and is not retained by the AI provider beyond the immediate request. We select AI providers that maintain strong data protection practices
  • Tax authorities: when you engage us for tax preparation services, we submit tax returns and related filings to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) and any applicable provincial tax authorities on your behalf
  • Professional advisors: we may share information with our legal, accounting, or other professional advisors as necessary to operate our business
  • Legal requirements: we may disclose your information when required by law, regulation, legal process, or enforceable governmental request
  • Business transfers: in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of the transaction, subject to the same privacy protections

4. data security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your information, including:

  • Encryption of data in transit using TLS/SSL protocols
  • Secure storage of financial documents and data
  • Access controls limiting who within our organization can view your data
  • Regular security assessments and updates
  • Secure authentication for portal access

While we take reasonable precautions to protect your information, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your data.

5. data retention

We retain your personal and financial information for as long as necessary to provide our services and comply with legal obligations. Specifically:

  • Active client data: retained for the duration of our service relationship and for a reasonable period thereafter to allow retrieval
  • Tax records: retained for a minimum of seven (7) years in accordance with CRA requirements
  • Financial documents: retained in accordance with applicable Canadian accounting standards and regulatory requirements
  • Account information: retained until you request deletion or for a reasonable period after service termination

Upon request, we will securely delete your data, subject to any legal retention obligations.

6. your rights

Under applicable Canadian privacy legislation, including the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and the British Columbia Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA), you have the right to:

  • Access: request a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Correction: request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete personal information
  • Deletion: request that we delete your personal information, subject to legal retention requirements
  • Withdraw consent: withdraw your consent to the collection, use, or disclosure of your personal information (which may affect our ability to provide services)
  • Complaint: file a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada or the BC Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided below.

7. cookies and local storage

The Service uses browser local storage to save your workflow progress within the client portal. We do not use third-party tracking cookies. Essential cookies may be used for authentication and session management.

8. children's privacy

The Service is not intended for use by individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately.

9. third-party links

The Service may contain links to third-party websites or services, including QuickBooks Online. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these third parties. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party services you access.

10. international data transfers

Your information may be processed and stored on servers located outside of Canada, including in the United States, where our third-party service providers (including cloud hosting, AI services, and QuickBooks Online) maintain facilities. When your data is transferred outside of Canada, it may be subject to the laws of the country in which it is stored. We ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your information in accordance with Canadian privacy law.

11. changes to this privacy policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of material changes by posting the updated policy on our website and updating the "last updated" date above. Your continued use of the Service after such changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
